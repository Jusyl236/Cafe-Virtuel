### **2. Model & Methods**

#### **2.1 Extended FitzHugh-Nagumo Dynamics**

The Mem4ristor v2.0 is built upon a three-variable extension of the FitzHugh-Nagumo oscillator model, incorporating constitutional doubt as a cognitive stabilizer. Each unit evolves according to:

```latex
\begin{align}
\frac{dv}{dt} &= v - \frac{v^3}{5} - w + I_{\text{ext}} - \alpha \tanh(v) + \eta_v(t) \\
\frac{dw}{dt} &= \varepsilon(v + a - bw) \\
\frac{du}{dt} &= \varepsilon_u(k_u \sigma_{\text{social}} + \sigma_{\text{baseline}} - u)
\end{align}
```

where $v$ represents the cognitive potential (opinion strength), $w$ is the recovery variable (inhibition), and $u$ is the constitutional doubt (epistemic uncertainty). The cubic nonlinearity is softened to $v^3/5$ (vs. standard $v^3/3$) to reduce explosion risk, while the term $-\alpha \tanh(v)$ introduces cognitive resistance preventing trivial saturation at extreme states.

#### **2.2 Anti-Uniformization Architecture**

The Mem4ristor v2.0 architecture prevents synchronization through three coupled mechanisms: attenuated social coupling ($D_{\text{eff}} \propto 1/\sqrt{N}$), constitutional doubt $u$ that filters conformity pressure via the $(1-u)$ term, and structural heretics (15% of units) with inverted coupling polarity, ensuring that complete uniformity is mathematically improbable even under strong stimuli.

The external input combines global stimulus with locally-scaled social coupling:

```latex
I_{\text{ext}} = I_{\text{stimulus}} + D_{\text{eff}}(1-u) \Delta v_{\text{Laplacian}}
```

where the effective coupling strength scales as $D_{\text{eff}} = D/\sqrt{N_{\text{size}}}$ to maintain consistent dynamics across network scales, and the Laplacian operator $\Delta v_{\text{Laplacian}} = \sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}(i)} (v_j - v_i)/|\mathcal{N}(i)|$ captures local conformity pressure from neighboring units.

Heretic units reverse the sign of their social coupling term:

```latex
I_{\text{ext,heretic}} = I_{\text{stimulus}} - D_{\text{eff}}(1-u) \Delta v_{\text{Laplacian}}
```

creating persistent counter-currents that maintain cognitive diversity by systematically opposing local consensus, with their influence modulated by network-wide doubt levels.

#### **2.3 Constitutional Doubt Dynamics**

The constitutional doubt variable $u$ evolves according to social stress:

```latex
\frac{du}{dt} = \varepsilon_u(k_u |\Delta v_{\text{Laplacian}}| + \sigma_{\text{baseline}} - u)
```

where $\sigma_{\text{baseline}} > 0$ guarantees activation even in isolation, and higher doubt exponentially reduces conformity pressure through the multiplicative $(1-u)$ filtering term, creating a negative feedback loop that stabilizes diversity. Social stress is defined as $\sigma_{\text{social}} = |\Delta v_{\text{Laplacian}}|$, directly measuring the magnitude of local opinion disagreement.

#### **2.4 Hardware-Realistic Noise**

To model fabrication imperfections in analog memristive implementations, additive Gaussian noise is injected on the cognitive potential:

```latex

```

with $\sigma_{\text{noise}} = 0.05$ corresponding to ~5% process variation observed in HfO‚ÇÇ-based memristive crossbars, turning device mismatch into a diversity-enhancing feature.
